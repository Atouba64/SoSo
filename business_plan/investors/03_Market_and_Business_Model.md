# 3. Market Analysis & Business Model

## Market Size
*   **Global Informal Savings:** Estimated at over $1 Trillion annually across Africa, Asia, and Latin America.
*   **African Diaspora Remittances:** $100 Billion+ annually. Much of this is sent to support families; SoSo redirects this flow into productive, rotating capital.
*   **South African Stokvels:** A $3+ Billion (R50B) market alone, with 11 million active participants.
*   **Francophone Africa (Beachhead):** High mobile money penetration, strong cultural affinity to Likelemba/Tontine, and significant diaspora in Europe (France/Belgium).

## Business Model
How SoSo makes money:
1.  **Transaction Fees:** A small flat fee or percentage (e.g., 1-2%) taken when the pot is paid out to a user.
2.  **Premium Subscriptions:** "SoSo Pro" for businesses or professional groups that require advanced features like bidding mechanisms (Bamileke style) or larger pot limits.
3.  **Liquidity Interest:** Late members pay a standard interest rate to the SoSo Liquidity Pool for covering their missed payment. 
4.  **Float Yield:** While funds sit in the escrow waiting for the cycle payout, SoSo can generate low-risk yield (subject to regulatory approval).

## Go-To-Market Strategy
1.  **Target the Diaspora First:** The diaspora has higher disposable income, bank accounts, and smartphones. We will target community leaders, religious organizations, and student groups in the UK/EU to form the first "Digital Likelembas".
2.  **Viral Group Mechanics:** A ROSCA is inherently viral. To start a SoSo, a user *must* invite 5 to 10 friends. Our CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) drops drastically due to this built-in network effect.
3.  **B2B2C Partnerships:** Partnering with existing informal groups (e.g., Church associations, market women cooperatives) to digitize their existing operations for better security and record-keeping.
